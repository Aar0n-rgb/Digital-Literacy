#Stay Safe Online – Simple Checklist for College Students (India)

- Use strong passwords (not “123456”)
  Make them a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Think of something only you would remember.
- Turn on 2-factor authentication:
  It might feel like an extra step, but it can save your accounts from getting hacked.
- Never share OTPs or passwords with anyone
  Not even if they say they’re from your bank or a “support team”—they’re not.
- Be cautious on public Wi-Fi
  College or café Wi-Fi is convenient, but avoid logging into banking or important accounts there.
- Don’t click random or suspicious links
  If a message says “You won a prize” or “urgent action needed,” it’s usually a trap.
- Pause and think before trusting online offers
  If someone is rushing you or the deal feels too good, take a moment—it probably is.
- UPI Tip: You NEVER need a PIN to receive money
  If someone asks you to enter your PIN to “get paid,” they’re trying to scam you.
- UPI Tip:  Double-check collect/payment requests
  Read carefully before approving one-tap can send money instead of receiving it.
- Keep your apps and phone updated
  Updates aren’t annoying, they fix security issues and keep you safe.
- Always log out from shared devices
  Using a friend’s phone or college computer? Don’t forget to log out.

# If something goes wrong act fast and report to National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in and call Helpline: 1930 as soon as possible to make sure that the scammer is caught.
