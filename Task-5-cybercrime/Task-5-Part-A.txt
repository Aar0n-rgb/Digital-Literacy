A UPI (Unified Payments Interface) scam is a type of digital fraud where criminals trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive details like UPI PINs or OTPs. These scams rely more on human manipulation than technical hacking.
Rohit, a college student, had posted an old phone for sale online. One evening, he received a call from someone claiming to be interested in buying it. The “buyer” sounded genuine and said he would send the payment through UPI. Instead of transferring money, the scammer sent a “collect request” and told Rohit to enter his UPI PIN to receive the payment. Trusting him and eager to complete the deal, Rohit followed the instructions. Within seconds, ₹15,000 was deducted from his account.
This is a common UPI scam where fraudsters manipulate people rather than hack systems. It usually begins with a call or message where the scammer pretends to be a buyer, bank official, or service provider. They create urgency or confusion and send a payment request or link. The victim, thinking they are receiving money, enters their UPI PIN or approves the request. In reality, they are authorizing a payment to the scammer.

Such scams often target students, online sellers, and people who are not fully aware of how UPI works. Since UPI transactions happen instantly, the money is gone almost immediately, making recovery very difficult.
Consequences:
Victims may lose money within seconds, often with little chance of recovery. Beyond financial loss, victims also experience stress, loss of trust in digital systems, and in some cases, identity theft. Beyond financial loss, victims feel embarrassed, stressed, and hesitant to trust digital payments again. Rohit learned the hard way that in UPI, you never need to enter your PIN to receive money.
